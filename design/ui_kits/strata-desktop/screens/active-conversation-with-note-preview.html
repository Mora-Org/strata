A lookahead that outlived its borrow

title A lookahead that outlived its borrow
depth L3 — apply
refs Klabnik & Nichols, ch.10
links borrow-checker-mental-model
created 2026-05-05 14:42

The borrow checker complaint in parse_expr isn't about memory — it's about scope. The mutable borrow taken inside the lookahead lives longer than the immutable borrow the caller still holds.

The shape of the problem

Two borrows, overlapping. The compiler can't prove they don't alias, so it refuses both. This is the safety guarantee doing its job: not protecting you from a crash, protecting you from a contract you never agreed to.

The fix

Take ownership of the token slice before the lookahead, instead of borrowing it across the call boundary. The lookahead then operates on data it owns, and the caller's borrow ends naturally.
